## Fill rules

- **Vocabulary first, before any substantive positioning work.** Each term gets exactly one definition that every function reads identically — apparent agreement often masks different underlying meanings, so write the definition down rather than assume it.
- **Surface same-word/different-meaning collisions.** Where two functions use the same word to mean different things (one person's "market" is another's "segment"; one person's "feature" is another's "value"), name the collision in the Notes column — do not paper over it.
- **Historical and founder assumptions baked into current positioning are the #1 obstacle — name them explicitly.** The current market category, feature terminology, target customer description, and lead use case are usually inherited from the product's conception and launch, not chosen because they are the best framing today. Labeling baggage out loud reduces its grip.
- For every Part B item, answer the driving question literally: best framing today, or "how we've always described it"? Record every "we've always done it this way" answer as baggage to set aside.
- **Default any inherited / "just habit" framing to Suspend.** Keep is only for framings the team can defend as the best choice *today*, independent of history.
- The team must **explicitly agree to suspend** old assumptions before moving to Step 4 — without that agreement, members anchor on existing assumptions and resist alternatives, undermining the process. Check the box only when that agreement is real.
