## Scope decision

> Decide what this list anchors before moving on. Pick exactly one.

- [ ] **Product-first** — single-product company: position product and company as the same thing.
- [ ] **Product-first** — multi-product, product-led entry: position the primary entry product first; company-level positioning comes later.
- [ ] **Company-first** — multi-product, bundle-led entry: position the company first; individual product positioning comes later.

**Scope chosen:** <product-first | company-first>
**Rationale:** <how many products are sold and how customers first encounter them>

## Fill rules

- Include **ecstatic / best-fit customers ONLY** — customers who understood the product fast, bought fast, became raving fans, and represent the type you want more of.
- Keep it a **short anchor list of 3–10 customers**, not the whole customer base; moderately satisfied customers dilute the pattern and must be excluded.
- **Remove** anyone who was slow to close, required heavy discounting, or whose use case is an edge case — they distort the signal.
- The "why" stays **customer-perceived** (why they bought, what they love), not a product-feature pitch.
- Scope decision is **exactly one** of product-first or company-first, per the single-product vs. multi-product/bundle rule.
- Set investor / pitch-deck framing aside; this list is the **customer-facing** anchor, not the investor narrative.
- This artifact is the **standing anchor** every later step references — keep it visible through Steps 2–10.
