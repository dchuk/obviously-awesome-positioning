## Fill rules

- This canvas **replaces** the traditional FOR / IS A / WHICH PROVIDES / UNLIKE positioning statement — do **not** add a fill-in-the-blank statement here. The book treats that template as an anti-pattern (it assumes the answers, reinforces status quo, and is never reused).
- Every field must **trace back to a completed earlier step** — this is consolidation, not discovery. If a cell would require inventing a new answer, return to the source template and finish that step first.
- Keep the clusters in component (2) limited to **2–5**, exactly as captured in competitive-alternatives.md — uniqueness and value claims are only meaningful relative to these real alternatives.
- An attribute in (3) earns its place only if it is **truly unique versus at least one cluster** in (2). A value theme in (4) earns its place only if it is backed by **objective proof** (data, customer/reviewer, or analyst opinion) — the company's own opinion is **not** valid proof; drop unprovable claims rather than soften them.
- The market category in (5) must put your strengths in their **best context**; record the assumptions it triggers and confirm they help rather than fight you. Choose exactly one positioning style: Head to Head, Big Fish Small Pond, or Create a New Game.
- The **relevant-trend row (+1) is optional** and used only when product strengths, market context, and customer interest genuinely overlap. A trend **supplements** the market category — it must never replace it (e.g. "AI" alone tells customers nothing about what the product is).
