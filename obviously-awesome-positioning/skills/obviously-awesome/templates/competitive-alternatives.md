## Fill rules

- Group alternatives into **2–5 clusters — no fewer, no more**. Fewer than 2 may signal an overly narrow best-fit customer definition; more than 5 means collapse the lowest-ranked or deprioritize edge cases.
- List alternatives as the customer **perceives** them, not from a product-centric or product-creator viewpoint.
- Always include **"do nothing" / status-quo and manual-process** options (spreadsheets, doing it by hand) — Dunford is explicit these are often the most common alternatives.
- Every cluster on the ranked table must pass the inclusion rule: a meaningful number of best-fit customers would actually use it and it has mindshare among prospects.
- Exclude low-mindshare alternatives (very few customers, edge-case-only, visible only to deep experts) — record each in the Edge cases section with its exclusion reason.
- Rank from **most common to least common** among best-fit customers. If you cannot rank with confidence, flag that customer interview data is still needed before positioning is finalized.
