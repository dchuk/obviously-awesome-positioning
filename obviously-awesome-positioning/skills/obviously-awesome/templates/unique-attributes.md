## Fill rules

- **Objective & provable attributes only.** No superlatives ("best-in-class", "easiest"), no benefit claims ("great support", "easy to use"). If it isn't an objective, measurable fact, rewrite it as the underlying capability or move it to the removal table.
- **Proof is non-negotiable.** Every attribute needs a Proof Source: a directly measurable fact (benchmark, count, certification) OR third-party validation (analyst report, independent review, approved customer quote). "Our team thinks so" does not count.
- **List everything, prioritize nothing.** Capture the broadest possible set — including weak candidates, attributes of uncertain value, and ones some team members see as negative. Do not rank or cluster yet.
- **Unique relative to named alternatives.** Each attribute must be something the Step 4 competitive alternatives do **not** have or do. Record which alternative(s) it is unique against.
- **Attributes live at product OR company level.** Product-level (features, integrations, benchmarks) and company-level (proprietary processes, expertise, partnerships, certifications, team) are both fair game.
- **Flag Consideration vs Retention.** Consideration attributes matter at purchase time and anchor positioning. **Retention** attributes (ongoing support, account management, ease of doing business) matter only after purchase — defer them to renewal/expansion messaging; they should not anchor external positioning.
