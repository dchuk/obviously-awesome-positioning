# Value Themes — Step 6 Output (input to Steps 7 & 8)

> **Purpose:** Builds on your unique attributes (Step 5) by mapping each attribute through the features→benefits→value chain, then clustering the value points into 1–4 customer-facing value themes; feeds Step 7 (target segmentation) and Step 8 (market frame of reference).

## Fill rules

- **1–4 themes maximum — no more, no fewer.** More than four means the grouping work isn't finished. A single theme is a valid, common outcome for tightly focused differentiation — do not force extra clusters.
- **Cluster from the customer's perspective**, by what customers relate to and which goals are related in their minds — NOT by internal engineering structure, codebase organization, or product-roadmap groupings.
- **Express themes in customer-goal language**, not feature or benefit language. If a theme still reads as a feature, cycle it back through the features→benefits→value chain until it names a goal the customer is trying to achieve.
- **Many-to-many is allowed.** One feature can map to multiple value points; a combination of features can yield value points no single feature provides alone. Capture all of them in Table 1.
- **Every theme must trace back to real unique attributes** from Step 5 — do not invent customer goals your attributes don't actually support.
- **At least one theme must be anchored in Consideration attributes.** Flag any theme supported exclusively by Retention attributes; those belong in renewal/expansion messaging, not core positioning.
