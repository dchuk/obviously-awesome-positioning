## Fill rules

- A **business owner must lead and be seen driving** the effort — CEO/founder for startups, division or business-unit leader for larger companies. Positioning is a business-strategy exercise, not a marketing task; if the owner is absent, the org will treat the output as a marketing document and never adopt it. Mark the sanity check STOP if this fails.
- **Marketing cannot "own" positioning** any more than it can own overall business strategy. Marketing is a participant, not the driver.
- Keep it **cross-functional**: sales, product/engineering, marketing, and customer success each hold knowledge no other function has (objections and alternatives, product reality, messaging, post-sale value and usage).
- Include **one or at most two** senior representatives per function. More than two from any one group risks it dominating discussion and drowning out quieter functions.
- Keep the team **small enough to actually decide** — three to twelve people have all worked; the real constraint is representation balance, not absolute size.
- Name a **facilitator (internal or external)** — an external facilitator is recommended because they have no investment in protecting historical decisions and can challenge untouchable assumptions; if internal, note how that person will stay neutral.
